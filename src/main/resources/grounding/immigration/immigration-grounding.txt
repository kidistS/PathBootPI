# Norwegian Immigration (UDI) – Domain Grounding Data
# Source: UDI – Norwegian Directorate of Immigration (www.udi.no) – for educational/demo use only

## OVERVIEW
UDI (Utlendingsdirektoratet) is the Norwegian Directorate of Immigration. It handles
applications for visas, residence permits, work permits, citizenship, and asylum.

---

## VISA

### Schengen Visa
- Citizens of many countries outside the EEA/EU require a Schengen visa for short stays
  (up to 90 days in 180 days) in Norway.
- Apply at the Norwegian embassy or consulate in your home country.
- Processing time: minimum 15 calendar days; often 2–6 weeks.
- Fees: approximately EUR 80 (adults).

---

## RESIDENCE PERMITS FOR NON-EEA CITIZENS

### Work Permit / Skilled Worker Permit
- Required before entry if you are a non-EEA/EFTA citizen who wants to work in Norway.
- You must have a concrete job offer from a Norwegian employer.
- The job must match the salary and working conditions of Norwegian collective agreements.
- Apply via Søknadsweb (udi.no/soknad) before arriving in Norway in most cases.

### Self-Employment Permit
- For non-EEA citizens who want to run their own business in Norway.
- Must demonstrate viable business plan and sufficient funding.

### Student Permit
- Must be enrolled in full-time study at an approved Norwegian institution.
- Applicants must document sufficient funds (approximately NOK 128,887/year, 2024).

### Family Immigration / Reunification
- Joining a spouse, cohabitant, or parent who is a Norwegian citizen or has a permanent
  residence permit.
- The reference person in Norway must meet income requirements
  (approximately NOK 321,751/year, 2024).

---

## EEA/EFTA CITIZENS

- EU/EEA/EFTA citizens have the right to enter, live, and work in Norway without
  a visa or residence permit for stays under 3 months.
- For stays longer than 3 months, they must register at the local police station.
- EU/EEA/EFTA family members of Norwegian citizens may also use these rights.

---

## PERMANENT RESIDENCE PERMIT

- Non-EEA citizens may apply after 3 years of continuous legal residence with a work
  or study permit (some permits require 5 years).
- Must pass a Norwegian language test or demonstrate sufficient Norwegian skills.
- Must not have been convicted of serious criminal offences.
- Must be self-sufficient (not dependent on social assistance).

---

## NORWEGIAN CITIZENSHIP

### Requirements
- Must have lived in Norway for at least 7 of the last 10 years with a residence permit.
- Must pass a Norwegian language test at level A2 or higher.
- Must not have serious criminal convictions.
- Must renounce previous citizenship (with some exceptions).
- Children under 18 can acquire citizenship together with a parent.

---

## ASYLUM

- Anyone who fears persecution in their home country can apply for asylum at the Norwegian
  border or at a police station inside Norway.
- UDI processes the application; the interview (asylintervju) is usually conducted within
  a few weeks of arrival.
- If granted protection, refugees receive a residence or settlement permit.
- Rejected asylum seekers can appeal to UNE (Utlendingsnemnda – Immigration Appeals Board).

---

## D-NUMBER (D-NUMMER)

- A temporary identity number issued to foreign nationals who do not qualify for a
  Norwegian national identity number (fødselsnummer).
- Required for banking, tax registration, and many public services.
- Apply at a tax office (Skatteetaten) or at certain police stations.

---

## INTEGRATION PROGRAM (INTRODUKSJONSPROGRAMMET)

- Mandatory 2-year (extendable to 3 years) integration program for newly arrived refugees
  and their family members.
- Includes full-time Norwegian language training and social science courses.
- Participants receive a weekly training grant (introduksjonsstønad) during the program.

## COMMON IMMIGRATION QUESTIONS

Q: I am from outside the EU/EEA. Can I work in Norway?
A: Yes, but you first need a work permit (tillatelse til å jobbe). You usually apply and
   receive a decision before you travel to Norway. You need a concrete job offer that
   meets Norwegian wage and working-condition standards.

Q: How long does it take to process a work permit?
A: Processing times vary: typically 2–8 weeks for skilled worker permits when applications
   are complete and submitted online.

Q: I just arrived as a refugee. What are my rights?
A: You have the right to accommodation in an asylum reception centre (asylmottak) while
   your case is processed. You may be allowed to work while waiting if processing takes
   more than 6 months. If granted protection, you enter the integration program.

Q: What documents do I need for a residence permit as a family member?
A: Passport, proof of relationship (marriage certificate, birth certificate), documentation
   that the reference person in Norway meets the income requirements, and completed
   application forms from udi.no.

Q: I have lived in Norway for 3 years on a work permit. Can I apply for permanent residency?
A: Generally yes, provided you have held a continuous work/residence permit, meet the
   income requirements, have not broken conditions of your permit, and can demonstrate
   sufficient Norwegian or Sami language skills.
