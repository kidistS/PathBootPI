# NAV – Norwegian Labour and Welfare Administration – Domain Grounding Data
# Source: NAV (www.nav.no) – for educational/demo use only

## OVERVIEW
NAV (Arbeids- og velferdsetaten) is the Norwegian Labour and Welfare Administration.
It manages approximately one-third of Norway's national budget through schemes such as
unemployment benefits, parental leave, sick pay, disability benefit, and pensions.

---

## UNEMPLOYMENT BENEFITS (DAGPENGER)

### Eligibility
- You must be registered as a job-seeker at NAV before receiving dagpenger.
- You must have earned at least NOK 177,394 in the last 12 months, OR NOK 354,788 in
  the last 36 months (2024 figures).
- You must be a genuine job-seeker and available for work.

### Amount
- 62.4% of your average income over the last 12 or 36 months, up to a maximum of
  6 × the National Insurance Basic Amount (Grunnbeløp, G).
- Maximum daily rate: approximately NOK 778 per day (2024).

### Duration
- Up to 104 weeks (approximately 2 years) if you earned more than 2G in the preceding year.
- Up to 52 weeks if you earned between 0.5G and 2G.

### How to Apply
1. Register as a job-seeker at nav.no.
2. Submit the dagpenger application online (søknad om dagpenger) at nav.no.
3. Send bi-weekly activity reports (meldekort).
4. Payment is made every two weeks.

## SICK PAY (SYKEPENGER)

### Employee Entitlement
- 100% of your salary from day 1 of sick leave, paid by your employer for the first
  16 calendar days (arbeidsgiverperioden).
- From day 17, NAV pays sykepenger up to 6G per year (≈ NOK 711,720 in 2024).
- Maximum duration: 52 weeks within a 3-year period.

### Self-Employed Sick Pay
- Self-employed persons are entitled to sykepenger from day 17 at 80% of their income
  (up to 6G), unless they have purchased additional insurance.

### Sick Note (Sykemelding)
- Medical certificate from a doctor is required if illness lasts more than 3 days
  (employees may use self-certification – egenmelding – for up to 3 days, or up
  to 24 days if the employer has joined the IA (Inclusive Workplace) scheme).

## PARENTAL LEAVE (FORELDREPENGER / SVANGERSKAPSPENGER)

- Total parental leave: 49 weeks at 100% pay OR 59 weeks at 80% pay.
- Mother's quota: 15 weeks (minimum); Father's/second parent quota: 15 weeks.
- Remaining weeks are shared.
- Both parents must have worked for 6 of the last 10 months before birth.

---

## DISABILITY BENEFIT (UFØRETRYGD)

### Eligibility
- Reduced work capacity by at least 50% due to illness or injury.
- Must have exhausted sick leave and work assessment allowance (AAP) first.

### Amount
- Approximately 66% of previous income (up to 6G).
- Disability benefit is taxable income.

---

## WORK ASSESSMENT ALLOWANCE (AAP – ARBEIDSAVKLARINGSPENGER)

- Bridge between sick pay and work or disability benefit.
- Requires that your work capacity is reduced by at least 50% due to illness.
- 66% of previous income, minimum NOK 285,836 per year (2024).
- Maximum duration: typically 3 years (extendable in special circumstances).

---

## OLD-AGE PENSION (ALDERSPENSJON)

- Flexible withdrawal from age 62 (if you have earned enough pension rights).
- Full state pension available from age 67.
- Pension depends on lifetime employment income; maximum is approximately 18.1% of
  annual income up to 7.1G per earning year.

---

## FINANCIAL SOCIAL ASSISTANCE (ØKONOMISK SOSIALHJELP)

Financial social assistance (sosialhjelp / økonomisk sosialhjelp) is a last-resort benefit
for people in acute financial need who cannot support themselves through work, other income,
or other NAV benefits such as dagpenger, AAP, or disability benefit.

### Who Can Get Financial Social Assistance
- Anyone in Norway in acute financial need who has no other means of support.
- It is means-tested: your income, savings, and assets are all assessed.
- You must first have applied for any other NAV benefits you may be entitled to.

### What It Covers
- Basic living expenses: food, housing, electricity, clothing, and other necessities.
- The amount is individually assessed based on your actual financial situation.

### How to Apply for Financial Social Assistance
1. Log in to nav.no with BankID or MinID.
2. Navigate to "Søk om økonomisk sosialhjelp" (Apply for financial social assistance).
3. Alternatively, visit your local NAV office in person – every municipality has one.
4. Bring or upload documentation: bank statements, income details, rent/bills.
5. A caseworker will review your application and may invite you to a meeting.
6. Normal processing time is 2–4 weeks; emergency (akutt) assistance can be faster.

### Where to Get Financial Social Assistance
- Apply online: https://www.nav.no/sosialhjelp/en
- Find your local NAV office: https://www.nav.no/kontaktoss/en

## COMMON NAV QUESTIONS

Q: How do I register as a job-seeker?
A: Log in at nav.no with BankID or MinID, go to 'Ditt NAV', and complete the job-seeker
   registration form. You must do this before applying for dagpenger.

Q: I just lost my job. What should I do first?
A: Register as a job-seeker at nav.no immediately, then submit a dagpenger (unemployment
   benefit) application. Deadline: you can apply up to 14 days before your last working day.

Q: As a new immigrant, am I entitled to NAV benefits?
A: You are generally entitled to benefits if you have a valid residence permit, a Norwegian
   D-number or national identity number, have been registered as a resident, and meet the
   income or contribution requirements specific to each benefit.

Q: What is meldekort?
A: Every two weeks you must submit a meldekort (activity/status report) confirming you are
   actively seeking work and available for employment. Failure to submit on time can suspend
   your payments.

Q: Can I work part-time while receiving benefits?
A: Yes, but you must report any income from part-time work to NAV. Your benefits may be reduced
 based on your earnings, and you must continue to meet the eligibility criteria for the benefits
 you are receiving. Always check with NAV before starting part-time work to understand how it may
 affect your benefits.

Q: How can I apply for financial social assistance?
A: You can apply for financial social assistance (sosialhjelp) online at nav.no by logging in with
   BankID or MinID and searching for "søk om økonomisk sosialhjelp". Alternatively, you can
   visit your local NAV office in person to apply. You will need to provide documentation of your
   financial situation, including income, expenses, and bank statements. The caseworker will
   review your application and may invite you for a meeting to discuss your needs. Processing time
   is typically 2–4 weeks, but emergency assistance may be available more quickly. More information:
   https://www.nav.no/sosialhjelp/en
