# Norwegian Tax (Skatteetaten) – Domain Grounding Data
# Source: Skatteetaten (www.skatteetaten.no) – for educational/demo use only

## OVERVIEW
The Norwegian Tax Administration (Skatteetaten) is responsible for collecting taxes
and for the National Population Register in Norway.

---

## INCOME TAX RATES (2024)

### Personal Tax Rates
- General income (alminnelig inntekt): 22% flat rate
- Low-income bracket: 0% (below NOK 70,000 / year)
- Bracket tax (trinnskatt) is applied progressively on top of general income tax:
  - Step 1: 1.7%  on income above NOK 208,050
  - Step 2: 4.0%  on income above NOK 292,850
  - Step 3: 13.6% on income above NOK 670,000
  - Step 4: 16.6% on income above NOK 937,900
  - Step 5: 17.6% on income above NOK 1,350,000

### National Insurance Contributions
- Employees pay 7.9% of gross income to national insurance (trygdeavgift).
- Self-employed pay 11.5%.

---

## STANDARD DEDUCTIONS

### Basic Allowance (Minstefradrag)
- 46% of income, capped at NOK 104,450 (2024)
- Minimum NOK 31,800

### Personal Allowance (Personfradrag)
- NOK 88,250 (standard for single taxpayers)

### Interest Deduction
- Interest paid on loans (mortgage, student loans, etc.) is deductible at 22%.

### Union Fee Deduction
- Up to NOK 7,700 per year in union membership fees is deductible.

### Home Office Deduction
- Standard home office deduction: NOK 2,040 per month if you work from home full-time.

---

## HOW TO FILE A TAX RETURN

1. The tax return is pre-filled (forhåndsutfylt skattemelding) every April.
2. Log in at skatteetaten.no using BankID or MinID.
3. Review all entries carefully – add any missing income or deductions.
4. Submit by 30 April (for employees) or 31 May (for self-employed).
5. Tax settlement (skatteoppgjør) is usually sent between June and October.
6. If you owe tax, the due date is 20 August / 25 September.
7. Overpaid tax is refunded directly to your bank account.

---

## TAX CARD (SKATTEKORT)

- A tax card tells your employer how much to withhold from your salary.
- Retrieve your tax card at skatteetaten.no or via the Skatteetaten app.
- Main card (Trekkpliktig A-inntekt) – used for primary employment.
- Secondary card (Biinntektskort) – used for second jobs; flat 50% rate.

---

## WEALTH TAX (FORMUESSKATT)

- Applied nationally (0.3%) and municipally (0.7%) on net wealth above NOK 1,700,000.
- Primary home valued at 25% of market value; secondary home at 100%.

---

## VALUE ADDED TAX (MVA / MOMS)

- Standard VAT rate: 25%
- Reduced rate (food and certain goods): 15%
- Low rate (passenger transport, hotel, cinema): 12%
- Exempt: health services, financial services, education

## COMMON TAX QUESTIONS

Q: What is the deadline for filing my tax return?
A: 30 April for employees; 31 May for self-employed.

Q: How do I get a tax refund?
A: If the pre-filled return is correct, and you are owed a refund, it is automatically
   paid after the tax settlement letter (skatteoppgjør) is issued (typically June–October).

Q: What documents do I need for a tax return as an immigrant?
A: D-number or national ID number (fødselsnummer), documentation of income from all sources
   (also from home country if applicable), bank interest certificates, and records of
   expenses you wish to deduct.

Q: Can I get a tax deduction for commuting?
A: Yes. If the distance between home and primary workplace exceeds 37 km per day,
   you can claim a commuting deduction of NOK 1.76 per km exceeding the threshold.

Q: As a new arrival, when am I liable for Norwegian tax?
A: You become a tax resident in Norway after staying 183 days in any 12-month period,
   or 270 days within a 36-month period.

Q: How do I change my tax card if I have multiple jobs?
A: Log in to skatteetaten.no, go to 'Tax deduction card', and select the appropriate card for each employer.
   The main card is for your primary job, and the secondary card is for additional jobs.
   The secondary card applies a flat 50% tax rate to ensure sufficient withholding.

Q: What are the items I can deduct from my taxable income?
A: Common deductions include:
- Basic allowance (minstefradrag)
- Personal allowance (personfradrag)
- Interest paid on loans
- Union fees
- Home office expenses (if applicable)
- Commuting expenses (if distance exceeds 37 km/day)
- Certain work-related expenses (if not reimbursed by employer)

Q: What are the newcomers exemptions for the first 3 years?
A: Newcomers to Norway may be eligible for a special tax exemption for the first three years of residence.
   This exemption allows you to exclude up to 50% of your income from Norwegian taxation, provided you meet certain criteria,
   such as having been a tax resident in another country for at least 10 of the last 15 years before moving to Norway.
   This can significantly reduce your tax liability during the initial years of residence in Norway.
   To apply for this exemption, you must submit a request to the Norwegian Tax Administration (Skatteetaten) and provide documentation of your previous tax residency.
   It is important to note that this exemption is not automatic, and you must actively apply for it to benefit from the reduced tax rate.
   Additionally, this exemption does not apply to income earned in Norway from Norwegian sources, such as employment or business income,
   which will still be subject to Norwegian taxation.
